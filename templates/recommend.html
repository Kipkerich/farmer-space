Nitrogen, Phosphorus, and Potassium

Soil N:P:K levels can be measured through soil testing, where samples are analyzed in a lab. This helps guide fertilization practices, ensuring optimal plant growth and soil fertility.

Rainfall, Temperature, and Humidity

These environmental factors significantly influence crop growth and productivity.

Rainfall

  • Dry area: Less than 500mm
  • Moderate area: 500mm - 1000mm
  • Wet area: Greater than 1000mm

Temperature

  • Very Hot: Above 35°C
  • Hot: 20°C - 30°C
  • Cold: 0°C - 20°C
  • Very Cold: Below 0°C

Humidity

  • Low: Below 30%
  • Moderate: 30% - 60%
  • High: 60% - 80%
  • Very High: Above 80%

Soil pH

Soil pH affects plant health and nutrient availability. Adjusting pH with lime (to raise) or sulfur (to lower) helps maintain optimal growing conditions.

  • Acidic Soil: 4 - 6.9
  • Neutral Soil: 7
  • Basic Soil: 7.1 - 10
